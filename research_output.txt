The Milky Way galaxy, which contains our Solar System, has a diameter of about 100,000 to 200,000 light-years. Its thickness is approximately 1,000 light-years, and it is estimated to contain 100 to 400 billion stars. The galaxy is a barred spiral shape and is just one of billions in the observable universe.
